WooCommerce + Keap: sync customers and purchase tags

Updated: 30 June 2026

WooCommerce holds your orders, subscriptions, and customer accounts. Keap (formerly Infusionsoft) holds your contacts, tags, and email automation. Without a bridge, you end up exporting CSVs, building fragile Zapier workflows, or manually tagging buyers after every product launch.

WP Fusion connects WooCommerce to Keap natively: when someone registers, purchases, renews a subscription, or abandons a cart, WP Fusion creates or updates the contact in Keap and applies tags you define. Your Keap automations then take over — welcome sequences, post-purchase follow-ups, win-back campaigns, and segmentation by lifetime value.

The problem with piecing it together manually

  • Duplicate contacts — same buyer registers on WordPress and again via a Keap form
  • Missing purchase context — Keap does not know what they bought unless you sync it
  • Zapier cost and limits — one Zap per event adds up; bi-directional field sync is harder
  • No abandoned cart in Keap — cart data stays in WooCommerce unless you push it

What WP Fusion syncs from WooCommerce to Keap

See the official WooCommerce documentation for full settings. Typical sync includes:

  • Customer records — create or update Keap contacts when users register or checkout
  • Purchase tags — tag by product, category, order status, or subscription event
  • Order and LTV fields — map order totals, product names, and lifetime value to Keap custom fields
  • Subscriptions — tag on subscribe, renew, cancel, or failed payment (WooCommerce Subscriptions)
  • Abandoned carts — sync cart contents for recovery campaigns in Keap

How setup works (high level)

  1. Install WP Fusion on WordPress and connect Keap using your API credentials (see the getting started guide).
  2. Enable the WooCommerce integration under WP Fusion → Integrations.
  3. Configure Apply Tags per event — e.g. tag Customer on any purchase, Bought Course Bundle for a specific product, or Subscription Active on renewal.
  4. Map contact fields under WP Fusion → Contact Fields so order data and LTV appear on the Keap contact record.
  5. Build Keap automations triggered by those tags — post-purchase emails, upsells, or sales follow-up tasks.

WooCommerce + Keap vs Zapier

Zapier works for simple one-way triggers (new order → add tag). WP Fusion is stronger when you need:

  • Bi-directional field sync between WordPress user meta and Keap custom fields
  • Tag-based content restriction on membership or course pages (same buyer may also use LearnDash — see our LearnDash tagging guide for a similar workflow with ActiveCampaign)
  • Many WooCommerce events (subscriptions, refunds, cart abandon) without maintaining separate Zaps
  • One contact record when the same person buys, learns, and submits forms

For a broader look at WordPress ↔ CRM bridges, see our WordPress + CRM integration overview — the same principles apply whether your CRM is Keap, ActiveCampaign, or HubSpot.

Setup checklist

  1. Connect Keap in WP Fusion (OAuth or API credentials per current Keap connection method).
  2. Enable WooCommerce integration and choose sync options (guest checkout, order statuses, etc.).
  3. Define tags per product or order event.
  4. Map order total, last product purchased, and LTV to Keap fields.
  5. Place a test order on staging → confirm contact, tags, and fields in Keap.
