Gravity Forms → CRM without Zapier

Updated: 7 July 2026

Gravity Forms is one of the most popular lead capture tools on WordPress. Your CRM (ActiveCampaign, Keap, HubSpot, Mailchimp, and others) is where follow-up automations, sales pipelines, and segmentation live. Without a direct connection, every form submission becomes a manual export, a brittle Zapier recipe, or a lead that never enters your email sequences.

WP Fusion integrates natively with Gravity Forms: each submission creates or updates a CRM contact, maps form fields to custom fields, applies tags, and can trigger automations — all from inside WordPress, with no per-submission Zapier costs.

The problem with Zapier-only Gravity Forms workflows

  • Per-task pricing — high-volume lead forms get expensive quickly on Zapier or Make
  • One-way sync — CRM tags rarely flow back to WordPress to personalize forms or unlock content
  • Fragmented logic — different tags for different form answers often mean separate Zaps and maintenance
  • No unified contact — the same person who submits a form, buys in WooCommerce, and joins MemberPress may exist as duplicate CRM records
  • Limited payment handling — syncing only successful Stripe or PayPal payments requires extra Zap steps

What WP Fusion does with Gravity Forms

See the official Gravity Forms documentation for full settings. Core capabilities include:

  • CRM feeds — add one or more WP Fusion feeds per form with field mapping and tags
  • Tagging leads — apply CRM tags (and lists, where supported) on every submission
  • Conditional logic — different feeds for different answers (e.g. “Enterprise” vs “Small business”)
  • Payment forms — process feeds only on successful payment, failed payment, or regardless of status
  • Quiz addon — sync quiz score, grade, pass/fail, and tag based on results
  • User Registration addon — sync new WordPress users created via Gravity Forms to your CRM
  • CRM-driven form fields — show or hide fields based on tags already on the contact
  • Auto-login & return after login — gate content behind a form opt-in, then redirect back to protected pages

How setup works (high level)

  1. Install WP Fusion on WordPress and connect your CRM (see the getting started guide).
  2. Create or edit a form in Gravity Forms and save it.
  3. Open Form Settings → WP Fusion and click Add New to create a feed.
  4. Map form fields to CRM custom fields using the + icon in the feed settings panel.
  5. Configure tags (and lists, if your CRM supports them) to apply when the form is submitted.
  6. Optionally enable Opt-in Condition for conditional logic — e.g. apply Enterprise Lead only when “Company size” is 50+.
  7. For payment forms, choose whether to process the feed on successful payment, failed payment, or always.
  8. Submit a test entry → confirm the contact, tags, and fields in your CRM. WP Fusion adds a note and metabox on each Gravity Forms entry with the CRM contact ID.

Multiple feeds and conditional logic

One Gravity Form can have several WP Fusion feeds. Use conditional logic so each feed runs only when specific fields match — for example, route “Support” inquiries to one tag set and “Sales” inquiries to another without building separate forms or Zaps.

If a submission did not sync correctly, open the entry in Gravity Forms and click Process WP Fusion actions again to re-send it to your CRM.

Gate content with a Gravity Forms opt-in

WP Fusion supports a common funnel: visitor tries to access protected content → redirect to a page with a Gravity Forms opt-in → submission creates a CRM contact and applies tags → auto-login unlocks the content → visitor returns to the page they wanted.

Enable Form Auto Login and Return After Login in WP Fusion settings, then protect the target page with required tags in the WP Fusion meta box. Full walkthrough: Gravity Forms integration docs (Return after login section).

Enhanced Ecommerce for paid Gravity Forms

With WP Fusion’s Enhanced Ecommerce addon, payment form submissions can sync to deal pipelines (HubSpot, HighLevel, Brevo) or ecommerce components (ActiveCampaign, Keap, Drip, Ontraport). Configure deal title, stage, and value on each feed — useful for consultation bookings, deposits, and productized services collected through Gravity Forms.

Gravity Forms + CRM vs Zapier

Zapier works for simple one-way triggers (new entry → add to list). WP Fusion is stronger when you need:

  • Multiple feeds with conditional logic on a single form
  • Payment-status-aware processing without extra Zaps
  • CRM tags controlling which form fields visitors see
  • Auto-login and content gating tied to form submissions
  • One contact record when the same person submits forms, buys in WooCommerce, and holds a membership
  • Batch export of historical Gravity Forms entries to your CRM

For a broader WordPress ↔ CRM overview, see our ActiveCampaign integration guide. Membership sites often combine lead forms with MemberPress — our MemberPress + CRM guide covers tag-based access. Store owners may also use WooCommerce + Keap sync for purchase tagging alongside form leads.

Setup checklist

  1. Connect your CRM in WP Fusion.
  2. Create a WP Fusion feed on each Gravity Form with field mapping and tags.
  3. Enable conditional logic if different answers need different tags or fields.
  4. For payment forms, set the correct payment-status processing rule.
  5. Submit a test entry → verify contact, tags, and entry notes in Gravity Forms.
  6. Optionally batch-export historical entries under WP Fusion → Advanced → Batch Operations.
