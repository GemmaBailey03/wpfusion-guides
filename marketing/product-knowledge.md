## One-line pitch

WP Fusion deeply integrates WordPress with your CRM or email marketing platform so you can tag users based on site activity, sync contact fields, control content access, and run marketing automation without custom code.

## Who it is for

- WordPress site owners using a CRM (ActiveCampaign, Keap/Infusionsoft, Mailchimp, HubSpot, etc.)
- Membership site operators (MemberPress, WooCommerce Memberships, Ultimate Member)
- Online course creators (LearnDash, LifterLMS, Tutor LMS)
- WooCommerce store owners who want CRM-driven email and segmentation
- Anyone tired of Zapier chains for basic WordPress ↔ CRM sync

## Core capabilities

### 1. Tag-based automation

When users take action on WordPress (purchase, form submit, lesson complete, login), WP Fusion applies tags in the connected CRM. Those tags trigger automations in the CRM (email sequences, SMS, sales tasks).

Official overview: https://wpfusion.com/features/

### 2. Content access control

Restrict pages, posts, courses, lessons, or downloads based on CRM tags. Users without required tags can be redirected (e.g. to a sales page).

Docs: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/getting-started/access-control/

### 3. Field sync (bi-directional)

Map WordPress user meta to CRM custom fields and keep data aligned. Display CRM field values inside WordPress.

Docs: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/getting-started/syncing-contact-fields/

### 4. Form → CRM

Send Gravity Forms, WPForms, Elementor Forms, Contact Form 7, Fluent Forms, and other form submissions to the CRM with tags and field mapping.

Example: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/lead-generation/gravity-forms/

### 5. Ecommerce tracking

Sync WooCommerce / EDD customers, orders, subscriptions, lifetime value, and purchase tags.

Example: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/ecommerce/woocommerce/

### 6. Abandoned cart

Track abandoned carts and sync to CRM for recovery campaigns.

Docs: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/abandoned-cart-tracking/abandoned-cart-overview/

### 7. Login / engagement tracking

Track last login, stale accounts, and site engagement in the CRM.

Docs: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/logins/overview

### 8. Email engagement in WordPress

Depending on CRM, sync email send/open/click activity so you can see engagement context alongside WordPress user records (CRM-dependent feature set).

### 9. LMS integrations

LearnDash, LifterLMS, Tutor LMS, LearnPress — tag on course enrollment, lesson completion, quiz results, etc.

LearnDash example: https://wpfusion.com/documentation/learning-management/learndash/

ActiveCampaign connect page: https://wpfusion.com/connect/connect-learndash-to-activecampaign/
